# Accessibility

The Visualizer includes controls and keyboard support for a range of visual and motion needs.

## Display controls

- **Font size** — use the **Aa −** and **+** controls to scale the interface.
- **High contrast** — strengthens foreground and boundary contrast.
- **Reduced motion** — minimizes animation and transitions. The application also respects your operating system's reduced-motion preference.

These settings are stored by your browser between visits.

## Keyboard navigation

- Move between controls with **Tab** and **Shift+Tab**. A visible **focus indicator** shows the active control.
- Activate a focused button or filter value with **Enter** or **Space**.
- Close dialogs, and exit or collapse the Selected Patients drawer, with **Escape**.

Specific interactions that are keyboard-operable:

- **Filter values** — focus a value and press **Enter** or **Space** to select or clear it.
- **Hierarchy groups** — the expand controls beside grouped values are focusable and can be toggled from the keyboard.
- **Patient dots** — focus a dot and press **Enter** or **Space** to open that patient.
- **Drawer tabs** — patient tabs and their close controls are reachable by keyboard.
- **Timeline points** — focus a document point and press **Enter** or **Space** to open it.
- **Patient Summary links and menus** — source-linked items and their document pickers are operable by keyboard.
- **Document mentions** — highlighted text mentions in the Document Viewer can be toggled by keyboard.

## Live updates

The patient count and result updates are announced to assistive technology as they change, so you are not left with a stale number.

## Accessibility Statement

An **Accessibility Statement** is available from **Home**. It records the current conformance:

- **Target:** WCAG 2.1 Level AA
- **Status:** Partially conformant

If a control is difficult to use with assistive technology, note the page, the control's label, your browser, and your assistive-technology version when reporting the issue.
